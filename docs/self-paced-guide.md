# Self-Paced Learning Guide

**Using the AI Coding Workshop Materials Independently**

## Welcome, Solo Learner!

This workshop was designed for in-person sessions, but all materials work perfectly for self-paced learning. You'll miss the energy of the room and peer learning, but you'll gain the ability to go at your own pace and dive deeper where you're interested.

## How to Use These Materials

### Time Commitment

**Full workshop:** 6-7 hours (spread over multiple sessions recommended)

**Suggested schedule:**
- **Session 1 (2 hours):** Module 1 - Foundations
- **Session 2 (1.5 hours):** Module 2 - Codebase Understanding
- **Session 3 (2 hours):** Module 3 - Full-Stack Features
- **Session 4 (1.5 hours):** Module 4 - Code Review & Security
- **Session 5 (1.5 hours):** Module 5 - Team Practices (adapt for solo)
- **Session 6 (1 hour):** Module 6 - Capstone

**Don't try to do it all in one day.** Your brain needs processing time.

### Prerequisites

Everything listed in [GETTING_STARTED.md](../GETTING_STARTED.md), plus:

**Self-directed learning skills:**
- Ability to stay focused without a facilitator
- Willingness to struggle through problems
- Comfort seeking help when stuck (online communities, docs)
- Self-accountability

## Solo Learning Strategies

### Create Accountability

**Option 1: Social accountability**
- Tweet your progress
- Blog about what you learned
- Post in coding communities
- Find a learning buddy online

**Option 2: Self accountability**
- Keep a learning journal
- Set completion goals
- Reward yourself for finishing modules
- Track time and progress

### Simulate Collaboration

**Techniques:**
1. **Rubber duck debugging** - Explain your code to an inanimate object
2. **Record yourself** - Present your work on camera
3. **Write it out** - Document as if teaching someone
4. **Challenge yourself** - "What would a code reviewer ask?"

### Avoid Solo Pitfalls

**Pitfall 1: Going too fast**
- Temptation: Skip exercises, just read
- Fix: Do ALL exercises, even when you "get it"

**Pitfall 2: No breaks**
- Temptation: Power through all modules
- Fix: Take 10-minute breaks between modules

**Pitfall 3: No reflection**
- Temptation: Jump to next exercise immediately
- Fix: Write down 3 takeaways before moving on

**Pitfall 4: Looking at solutions too early**
- Temptation: Check solution when stuck
- Fix: Struggle for 20 minutes first

**Pitfall 5: Isolation**
- Temptation: Never ask for help
- Fix: Use online communities, Stack Overflow, forums

### When to Get Help

**Stuck on setup?**
- Read [GETTING_STARTED.md](../GETTING_STARTED.md) troubleshooting
- Search GitHub issues
- Ask in online communities

**Stuck on an exercise?**
- Re-read the requirements
- Check the hints
- Struggle for at least 20 minutes
- Then check solutions or ask for help

**Conceptual confusion?**
- Read the docs thoroughly
- Search for external resources
- Ask AI to explain (but don't just copy)
- Online communities are friendly!

## Making the Most of Solo Learning

### Advantages You Have

**Flexibility:**
- Go at your own pace
- Deep dive where interested
- Skip or skim what you already know
- Pause and research tangents

**Focus:**
- No distractions from others
- Control your environment
- Schedule sessions when you're most productive

**Customization:**
- Adapt exercises to your interests
- Use your own tech stack
- Solve problems your own way

### Challenges You'll Face

**Motivation:**
- No peer pressure to complete
- Easy to procrastinate
- No facilitator to guide

**Solutions:**
- Set specific completion dates
- Tell someone you're doing this
- Join an online study group
- Treat it like a real class

**Isolation:**
- No peer learning
- No immediate feedback
- Lonely struggle

**Solutions:**
- Find an online buddy
- Share progress publicly
- Join coding communities
- Talk to yourself (seriously!)

## Alternative Formats

### Study Group (2-4 people)

If you can gather a few colleagues:
1. Meet weekly to complete one module
2. Do exercises individually
3. Come together to discuss
4. Present your solutions to each other
5. Do the demos together

**Benefits:**
- Peer accountability
- Different perspectives
- Collaborative learning
- More fun!

### Mentor-Mentee Pairing

If you have a mentor or are mentoring:
1. Mentee does exercises
2. Mentor reviews solutions
3. Discuss approaches
4. Mentor shares real-world context

**Benefits:**
- Personalized feedback
- Career guidance
- Relationship building
- Deeper learning

## After Completion

### Apply Your Learning

1. **Use AI tools in your daily work**
   - Start with low-risk tasks
   - Build confidence gradually
   - Track your productivity

2. **Share with your team**
   - Present what you learned
   - Propose team guidelines
   - Run an internal workshop

3. **Continue Learning**
   - AI tools evolve rapidly
   - Keep experimenting
   - Stay updated on best practices

### Give Back

**Help future learners:**
- Contribute improvements to this repo
- Write about your experience
- Answer questions in communities
- Mentor someone else

## Final Thoughts

**Solo learning is hard but rewarding.**

You won't have the energy of a live workshop, but you'll have deeper control over your learning. Take your time. Struggle with the exercises. Ask for help when needed. And most importantly: **actually do the work.**

Reading about AI coding tools isn't the same as using them. **Ship code. Make mistakes. Learn.**
